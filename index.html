   About Me

 

Welcome to my virtual abode, fellow Internaut!

Hello there! My name is Shail.

I'm a current undergrad Electrical Engineering student in 2B at the University of Waterloo.

My passion for Engineering began at an early age. As a child, I found myself spending long hours constantly breaking things to see what was happening on the insides. I would also try fixing things but moreso the former than the latter. I like to get my hands dirty with Electronics and Embedded Software development projects. I like understanding how a particular system works and figuring out how I can potentially change it to suit my needs and improve its functionality. The end goal being to engineer innovation.

   Work Experience

 


While at Facebook, I worked on the Server Infrastructure Hardware Group as part of the Power Team. As a Power Engineering Intern, I worked on developing the core power design and validation skills. My main project was to develop a debug card that is currently being used to field test Power Shelves. I also had the opportunity to contribute towards the schematics design of next generation power supplies and battery backup units.





Working at SMART allowed me to develop a wide range of skills from firmware development, Printed Circuit Board layout design, designing Schematics, rapid prototyping, to assembling my very own PCB.
Over the course of my 4 month term, I was independently involved in creating a prototype based on an Atmel MCU and Bluetooth Low Energy, in all phases from concept creation to the pre-production prototype.








As a Senior Director, I helped run the Fall 2013 Waterloo Engineering Competition (WEC) at the University. The WEC is the stepping stone for students to expand and share our vision amongst other schools across Canada. My primary responsibilities included developing the problem statement, organizing judges, developing prototype solutions, and hosting the actual competition together with other Executives from Waterloo's Engineering Society.
